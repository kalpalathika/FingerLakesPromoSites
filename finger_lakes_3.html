History

Around two million years ago, a series of glacial flows began moving seasonally across what is now New York State. These enormous ice formations traveled southward, carving deep trenches, grinding through valleys, and reshaping the landscape. Their passage left behind long, narrow, steep-walled lakes and deep gorges. The term "finger lake" refers to these elongated lakes formed in glacially over-deepened valleys. Among them, Cayuga and Seneca Lakes stand out as some of the deepest in the United States, with depths of 435 feet and 618 feet, respectively. Remarkably, their lake bottoms lie well below sea level.

Landscape

Glacial Heritage

The Finger Lakes were sculpted by massive glaciers over two million years ago. These natural forces carved deep valleys, leaving behind stunning long, narrow lakes surrounded by steep walls and lush greenery.

Scenic Lakes

Cayuga and Seneca Lakes are highlights of the Finger Lakes region, known for their extraordinary depths 435 feet and 618 feet, respectively. Their crystal-clear waters and tranquil shores create breathtaking landscapes.

Gorge Trails

The region is home to dramatic gorges, waterfalls, and rugged cliffs. Popular spots like Watkins Glen State Park offer hiking trails that weave through striking rock formations and cascading waterfalls.

Seasonal Beauty

The Finger Lakes landscape transforms with each season from vibrant wildflowers in spring to golden autumn foliage, snowy winter vistas, and serene summer days, making it a year-round destination for nature lovers.
